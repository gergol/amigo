# 01 — Curriculum: Module Progression A1/A2

The curriculum is a sequence of **modules**. Each module bundles:

- **Grammar points** — the atomic units of the grammar graph. Each has an ID and prerequisites; unlocking a module unlocks its grammar points.
- **Vocab theme** — a thematic vocab unit (fed to the vocab trainer).
- **Template unlocks** — new templates become available; *existing* templates gain new variation axes (e.g. once *indefinido* is unlocked, every eligible action template can also be asked in the past).

Scope follows the [PCIC grammar inventory A1–A2](https://cvc.cervantes.es/ensenanza/biblioteca_ele/plan_curricular/niveles/02_gramatica_inventario_a1-a2.htm); ordering follows common textbook practice (roughly the order German learners meet things in courses).

## A1 modules

### M01 — Identity: ser + nouns & articles
- Grammar: subject pronouns (drop-able!), `ser` presente, noun gender & plural, definite/indefinite articles, adjective agreement (basics), nationality/profession without article (*soy médico*)
- Vocab: greetings & courtesy, countries & nationalities, professions, family
- Templates: *X es/soy … (name, origin, profession, relation)*, questions with *quién/qué/de dónde*
- 🇩🇪 Notes: subject-pronoun dropping is new (German always has the pronoun); no article before professions (*ich bin **ein** Arzt* → *soy médico*); grammatical gender rarely matches German (*das Buch* → *el libro*)

### M02 — Location & existence: estar + hay
- Grammar: `estar` presente, `hay` vs. `está(n)`, prepositions of place (*en, sobre, debajo de, al lado de…*), contractions `al`/`del`
- Vocab: home & furniture, city places
- Templates: *¿Dónde está X?*, *Hay un/una X en…*, *X está + place*
- 🇩🇪 Notes: **first ser/estar split** — but kept easy here: identity vs. location. *hay* ≈ *es gibt* maps well. `al`/`del` ≈ *zum/vom* is a friendly analogy.

### M03 — Regular present tense
- Grammar: presente of regular *-ar/-er/-ir*, negation with *no*, question words (*qué, dónde, cuándo, cómo, por qué, cuánto*), intonation questions + ¿…?
- Vocab: daily activities (hablar, comer, vivir, trabajar, estudiar…), numbers 0–100, days of the week
- Templates: subject + verb + object/place/time; negated variants; wh-questions
- 🇩🇪 Notes: three conjugation classes vs. German's essentially one; single negation word position (*no* before verb).

### M04 — Key irregular verbs & near future
- Grammar: `tener, ir, hacer, venir, salir, poner, ver, saber, decir` presente; *tener* idioms (*tener X años, hambre, sed, frío*); `ir a + infinitivo`; *hay que / tener que + inf*
- Vocab: time & clock, months/seasons, everyday places (*banco, escuela, mercado*)
- Templates: plans (*voy a…*), obligation (*tengo que…*), age/state via tener
- 🇩🇪 Notes: *tener años* vs. *ich **bin** X Jahre alt*; *tener hambre* vs. *hungrig sein* — the "haben statt sein" pattern deserves focused drilling. *ir a + inf* ≈ *werden* / *vorhaben*.

### M05 — Stem-changing verbs
- Grammar: e→ie (*querer, preferir, empezar, entender*), o→ue (*poder, dormir, volver, costar*), e→i (*pedir*), u→ue (*jugar*); modal-like *querer/poder + inf*
- Vocab: food & drink, restaurant, shopping basics
- Templates: ordering, preferences, ability (*¿puedo…?*), prices (*¿cuánto cuesta?*)
- 🇩🇪 Notes: stem change only in stressed syllables (1./2. plural keep the vowel) — the "boot pattern" needs explicit drilling; German has vowel changes too (*du schläfst*) which helps acceptance.

### M06 — Reflexives & daily routine
- Grammar: reflexive verbs (*llamarse, levantarse, ducharse, acostarse, vestirse*), reflexive pronoun placement, time-of-day expressions, frequency adverbs (*siempre, normalmente, nunca*)
- Vocab: daily routine, hygiene, clothing
- Templates: routine narration (*me levanto a las siete y…*), habits with frequency
- 🇩🇪 Notes: pronoun goes *before* conjugated verb (*me llamo* vs. *ich nenne **mich***); many Spanish reflexives aren't reflexive in German (*levantarse* = *aufstehen*).

### M07 — Gustar & friends
- Grammar: `gustar/encantar/interesar/doler` with indirect-object pronouns, singular/plural agreement with the *thing liked*, prepositional pronouns (*a mí, a ti, conmigo, contigo*) for emphasis/contrast, *también/tampoco*
- Vocab: hobbies & free time, sports, music, body parts (for *doler*)
- Templates: likes/dislikes with person × liked-thing × plurality variation; agreeing/disagreeing
- 🇩🇪 Notes: **the great bridge**: *me gusta* maps 1:1 to *mir gefällt* — teach it via *gefallen*, not *mögen*, and the inverted construction is instantly familiar. *doler* ≈ *wehtun (mir tut … weh)*, same pattern.

### M08 — Ser vs. estar with adjectives
- Grammar: ser = inherent trait, estar = state/condition; the meaning-shifting adjectives (*aburrido, listo, rico, malo, bueno*); *muy* vs. *mucho*; weather with *hace/está/hay*
- Vocab: character & appearance adjectives, moods & feelings, weather
- Templates: minimal pairs are first-class here — *Ich bin gelangweilt → Estoy aburrido* vs. *Ich bin langweilig → Soy aburrido*; person descriptions; weather small talk
- 🇩🇪 Notes: **the single hardest A1 topic for German speakers** (German has only *sein*). The German prompt always disambiguates naturally because German uses *different words* for the two readings (see 02-Lexicon).

### M09 — Progressive & direct objects
- Grammar: `estar + gerundio` (incl. irregular gerunds *leyendo, durmiendo*), direct-object pronouns (*lo/la/los/las*), **personal a**, pronoun placement (before conjugated verb / attached to infinitive)
- Vocab: colors, more shopping, tech & communication basics
- Templates: what's happening now; *¿Ves a María? — Sí, la veo.*
- 🇩🇪 Notes: German has no progressive — map to *gerade* (*ich lese gerade*); **personal a has no German counterpart at all** and needs generous drilling; DO pronoun before the verb (*lo veo* vs. *ich sehe ihn*).

### M10 — Determiners & first comparisons
- Grammar: possessives (*mi/tu/su/nuestro…*), demonstratives (*este/ese/aquel*), basic comparatives (*más/menos … que, tan … como*), ordinal numbers 1–10, dates
- Vocab: celebrations & invitations, review-and-mix theme
- Templates: comparing people/things, whose-is-what, invitations with dates
- 🇩🇪 Notes: three-way demonstratives vs. German two-way; comparative always analytic (*más grande*, never a *-er* form; irregulars *mejor/peor/mayor/menor*).

**A1 exit state:** learner can generate present-tense sentences across all template families, with reflexives, gustar, object pronouns, and correct copula choice.

## A2 modules

### M11 — Pretérito perfecto
- Grammar: *haber* presente + participio, irregular participles (*hecho, visto, escrito, dicho, puesto, vuelto*), markers *ya / todavía no / alguna vez / hoy / esta semana*, *acabar de + inf* for the immediate past (*acabo de llegar* ≈ *ich bin gerade angekommen*)
- Vocab: travel & holidays, experiences
- Templates: what have you done today/ever; all eligible action templates gain the *perfecto* axis
- 🇩🇪 Notes: near-perfect structural match to German Perfekt — **but** only *haber* (never *ser* as auxiliary: *ist gegangen* → *ha ido*), and the participle **never agrees** and nothing splits the verb bracket.

### M12 — Pretérito indefinido (regular)
- Grammar: regular *-ar/-er/-ir* indefinido, markers *ayer, anoche, la semana pasada, en 2019, hace dos años*
- Vocab: biography & life events, past time expressions, numbers above 100 & years (*mil novecientos…, dos mil…*)
- Templates: what happened yesterday/last week; narration in sequence (*primero…, después…, luego…*)
- 🇩🇪 Notes: functionally ≈ Präteritum but used in *speech* (unlike German). Accent marks distinguish forms (*hablo* vs. *habló*) — first place where accents change meaning.

### M13 — Indefinido irregulars
- Grammar: *ser/ir* (identical!), *estar, tener, hacer, poder, poner, venir, querer, decir, traer, dar, ver*; stem+ending system of the "strong" preterites (no accents)
- Vocab: trips & anecdotes (continues M12 theme)
- Templates: same as M12 with irregular verbs weighted in
- 🇩🇪 Notes: *fui* = both "ich war/ging" — context disambiguates; drill as a closed high-frequency set, like German strong verbs.

### M14 — Imperfecto
- Grammar: imperfecto (only 3 irregulars: *ser, ir, ver*), uses: habitual past (*antes…*), descriptions, age/time/weather in the past
- Vocab: childhood, school memories, describing how things were
- Templates: *De niño/a…* habits; past descriptions of people and places
- 🇩🇪 Notes: German doesn't grammatically distinguish habitual/descriptive past — introduce imperfecto **alone** first (this module) before contrasting.

### M15 — Indefinido vs. imperfecto
- Grammar: the contrast: background vs. event, interrupted actions (*imperfecto cuando indefinido*), *mientras*
- Vocab: accidents & incidents, storytelling connectors
- Templates: two-clause narrative frames (*Estaba duchándome cuando sonó el teléfono*); copula choice in the past (ser/estar × two past tenses)
- 🇩🇪 Notes: **the hardest A2 topic for German speakers** — no German equivalent distinction. German prompt disambiguates via *gerade/immer/plötzlich, als/während* cues; template design must plant these cues deliberately.

### M16 — Future & polite wishes
- Grammar: futuro simple (incl. *tendré, haré, podré, saldré…*), predictions vs. *ir a + inf* for plans, *me/te/le gustaría + inf*
- Vocab: weather forecast, plans & resolutions, appointments
- Templates: forecasts, promises, polite wishes (*me gustaría reservar…*)
- 🇩🇪 Notes: one-word future vs. *werden + Inf*; *gustaría* taught as fixed chunk (conditional proper is B1).

### M17 — Imperative & directions
- Grammar: imperativo afirmativo (*tú, usted, vosotros, ustedes*), key irregulars (*pon, haz, ve, ven, di, sal, sé, ten*), pronoun attachment (*dímelo, levántate*) with accent shift (negative imperative is B1, out of scope)
- Vocab: directions & city navigation, house rules, recipes
- Templates: giving directions, instructions, recipes
- 🇩🇪 Notes: pronouns glue onto the imperative (vs. German *sag es mir*); accent added to keep stress — first systematic look at written accent rules.

### M18 — Double object pronouns
- Grammar: IO + DO combined, *le/les → se* before *lo/la*, placement across conjugated verb / infinitive / gerund / imperative
- Vocab: gifts & favors, services
- Templates: *¿Me lo das? — Sí, te lo doy.* — high-variation pronoun drills over known verbs
- 🇩🇪 Notes: order is IO-before-DO, opposite of German default (*ich gebe **es dir*** → *te lo doy*); *se lo* substitution has no German analogue.

### M19 — Comparison, adverbs, habits
- Grammar: superlatives (*el más… / -ísimo*), full comparative system, adverbs in *-mente*, *soler + inf*, quantity indefinites (*algo/nada/alguien/nadie/alguno/ninguno*)
- Vocab: describing & rating things, opinions
- Templates: rating and comparing (3-way: X > Y > Z), habits with *soler*
- 🇩🇪 Notes: double negation is grammatical and required (*no veo nada* vs. *ich sehe nichts*).

### M20 — Connectors & complex sentences
- Grammar: *porque, cuando + presente, si + presente (real conditions), que*-clauses after *creo/pienso/sé* (indicative), relative clauses with *que* (*la chica que vive aquí*), *desde / desde hace / hace … que*, *antes de / después de + inf*
- Vocab: health & at the doctor's, feelings & opinions
- Templates: two-clause templates combining any unlocked tense pair; *Seit wann…? — desde hace…*
- 🇩🇪 Notes: *seit* splits into *desde* (point) vs. *desde hace* (duration) — classic German-speaker error; no subjunctive yet (B1) — *creo que* templates deliberately stay indicative.

**A2 exit state:** learner generates and understands sentences across five tenses + imperative, with full pronoun system, correct copula in all tenses, and two-clause narration.

## Grammar point graph

Every grammar point gets an ID (`presente.regular.ar`, `copula.estar.location`, `pron.do.placement`, `past.indefinido.irregular`, …) and a prerequisite list. Templates reference these IDs. The module structure above is the *default path* through the graph — but points can also be marked known directly in the knowledge settings (see 00): checking a point auto-checks its prerequisites, unchecking one unchecks its dependents. Downstream consumers (templates, drills) only see the resulting set of unlocked points, never *how* they were unlocked.

Rule of thumb for granularity: a grammar point is the smallest unit that a template could require *independently*. (E.g. `perfecto.participio.irregular` is separate from `perfecto.regular`, so early perfecto templates can exclude irregular participles.)
